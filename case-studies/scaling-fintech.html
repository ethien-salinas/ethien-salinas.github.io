Building a Fintech Platform for Migrants in Europe

How I helped take Íkualo from an early idea to an operating fintech platform, building the technology function, evolving the product, and preparing the company for scale.

Role: CTO Company: Íkualo Stage: Idea → Launch → Scale-up Focus: Platform, product, org design
Starting Point
Idea stage with no product or technical roadmap
Initial Tech Setup
One technical role at the beginning
Current Tech Org
15 internal people plus specialized external support
Outcome
Operating fintech platform with thousands of users

Íkualo started with a strong mission and very limited structure. My work began by helping define the technical path, evaluating providers, and shaping the first realistic route to market. It later evolved into building the internal technology organization, strengthening platform ownership, and preparing the company for scale.

Context

Íkualo was founded with the vision of becoming a financial platform designed specifically for Latin American migrants living in Europe. The objective was to simplify access to financial services while operating within the legal and regulatory frameworks required in Europe.

I met Harold Correa, the founder and CEO, when the company was still at the idea stage. He had already explored the opportunity and was looking for someone with fintech technical experience who could help transform the concept into a viable product and technology platform.

At the beginning, the company consisted of a very small team: the CEO, one person in marketing, one in design, one in PR, and me as the only technical figure.

The Challenge

The main problem was not a failing system, but the absence of a clear execution path. There was a strong business idea, but no concrete roadmap for how to launch, how to structure the product, or how to make the platform technically viable.

  • Define how to launch a compliant fintech platform in Europe.
  • Evaluate and integrate Banking-as-a-Service providers.
  • Launch quickly while avoiding long-term product limitations.
  • Build a foundation that could grow beyond a single banking feature set.

My Role

I joined as CTO and initially played a hybrid role across technical strategy, vendor evaluation, delivery coordination, and product definition.

  • Evaluated Banking-as-a-Service providers and supporting fintech infrastructure.
  • Created diagrams and explanations so business decisions could be made with technical clarity.
  • Coordinated implementation with providers during the first phase.
  • Built the internal technology function as the company matured.
  • Actively contributed to product prioritization based on feasibility and business impact.

Strategy

The initial strategy optimized for speed to market. The first version of the platform relied on a white-label approach that made early launch possible without having to build everything from scratch.

As the product matured, the limitations of that model became more visible: less flexibility, slower iteration, and dependency on external provider priorities. The strategy then shifted toward progressively building more internal ownership and extensibility.

Phase 1

Reach the market quickly

Use available fintech infrastructure and provider-led delivery to launch fast and validate the proposition.

Phase 2

Reduce dependency

Move from a white-label dependency toward internal services and stronger control over product evolution.

Phase 3

Prepare the platform for scale

Strengthen architecture, team structure, and product capabilities to support growth and future financial services.

Platform Architecture

The platform evolved toward a cloud-native architecture designed for flexibility, compliance, and integration with specialized providers.

Core stack

  • AWS cloud infrastructure
  • Node.js / NestJS backend
  • Microservices architecture
  • React Native mobile application
  • Docker and Kubernetes

Key integrations

  • Banking-as-a-Service provider
  • KYC provider
  • SMS, email, WhatsApp, and push notifications
  • Apple Wallet
  • Google Wallet

Product Capabilities

The result was a customer-facing product with a fully digital onboarding and account opening experience.

  • 100% digital onboarding and KYC
  • Biometric registration for login
  • Spanish IBAN account
  • Card request from the start of the journey
  • Apple Wallet and Google Wallet connectivity
  • SCA and password management policies as part of the security baseline

Building the Engineering Organization

Team evolution

  • Started with me as the only technical role
  • Then added 3 full-stack developers
  • Later incorporated UX/UI and DevOps support

Current structure

  • Architect
  • UX/UI
  • QA

  • Full-stack engineers
  • DevOps (external)

Company Growth

The company evolved from the original five-person setup into a broader organization covering leadership, operations, marketing, customer support, and technology.

  • General management
  • Administration and finance
  • Marketing with 3 internal people and one external agency
  • Customer support with 8 people covering shifts and schedules
  • Technology with 15 internal people and specialized third-party support such as security reviews

Results

  • Took the company from idea stage to an operating fintech platform.
  • Helped launch a product serving thousands of users.
  • Built the internal technology function from scratch.
  • Created the technical foundation for expansion beyond the initial account product.
  • Supported the transition from startup execution to scale-up readiness.

Difficult Decisions

Some of the hardest decisions were related to people and organizational maturity.

As the company evolved, some profiles that had been promising at the beginning were no longer the right fit for the next stage. Early startup environments often reward energy and willingness, but scale requires stronger ownership, broader systems thinking, and higher execution consistency.

Another key lesson came from hiring strategy. Initially, it seemed financially prudent to lean more heavily on junior profiles. Over time, that proved more expensive than expected: slower delivery, more basic mistakes, weaker architectural judgment, and more exposure to risk in a regulated environment.

Shifting toward stronger seniority improved both velocity and quality, while also reducing operational and technical risk.

Key Learnings

Building a fintech company happens in stages: from idea to product, from product to validation, and from validation to scale. Each stage requires different decisions, different talent, and different leadership behaviors.

Security and regulatory compliance are often invisible when things go well, but they are fundamental. Very few people highlight them proactively, yet everyone expects them to be fully under control when something goes wrong.

I also learned that architecture and organization need to evolve together. The platform can only scale sustainably if the team, the operating model, and the product sequencing mature at the same pace.

CTO Perspective

Building a fintech platform is not only a technology challenge. It is also an organizational and product challenge. The real work is making architecture, people, and business priorities evolve together.

— Ethien Salinas
