Product Ownership Through Execution

How I translate ambiguity into real products by combining technical depth, delivery discipline, and product ownership across fintech and early-stage environments.

Focus: Product ownership Angle: Execution-driven Context: Fintech & early stage Profile: Product, technology, delivery
Approach
Turn abstract ideas into first real versions
Background
Technology, fintech, and project execution
Experience
Creze, Intelligential, and Íkualo
Fit
Product Owner, Founding Product Lead, CTO/Product crossover

I am not a traditional Product Manager. My product perspective comes from years of building and launching technology in startups where product, engineering, operations, and business were deeply intertwined. My strength is turning ambiguity into a first version that can be launched, used, and improved methodically.

How I Think About Product

For me, building a product means moving from an idea to a working version within the constraints of reality. That includes team capability, budget, time-to-market, regulatory requirements, operational complexity, and opportunity cost.

A good first version is not the one with the most features. It is the one that enables the business to start moving. Once a product exists in the real world, feedback becomes more useful than theoretical discussions.

What My Technical Background Adds

Because I have spent years building and operating systems, I understand what it actually takes to implement ideas. That changes how I work on product: I do not just define features, I think through architecture, data, operations, support, and future evolution.

What this enables

  • Better alignment with engineering teams
  • Awareness of architectural implications
  • Data models designed to support analytics from the start
  • More realistic prioritization and delivery sequencing

Why it matters

  • Features are not defined in isolation
  • Analytics cannot be an afterthought
  • Operational teams need workable product flows
  • Compliance and traceability shape the product itself

My Product Framework

When working with an ambiguous idea, I typically move through a structured process that turns abstraction into something tangible and reviewable.

Idea | v User Flow Definition | v Feature List | v System Requirements | v Data Model & Analytics Considerations | v First Product Version | v Feedback and Iteration

How I Operate in Practice

I usually start with user flows because they are easier for stakeholders to understand than internal system logic. Once the flow is clear, I translate it into the features, requirements, and system considerations needed to launch.

How I structure the work

  • Define user flows first
  • Break each step into features and requirements
  • Add system requirements and contextual dependencies
  • Consider data models early, including analytics needs

How I prioritize and learn

  • Prioritize end-user value first
  • Then support internal operational needs
  • Use project management dependency logic where needed
  • Learn from event dashboards and customer support feedback

Customer support is especially valuable once a product is live. Their sensitivity to user friction is often one of the most practical feedback sources in the company.

Where I Have Applied This Approach

Creze

Working capital lending platform

At Creze, I participated in defining and evolving the product platform supporting the lending process.

  • Developed the origination product that captured customer applications
  • Enabled the credit risk team to evaluate and decide on loan approval
  • Integrated with a credit management platform used to operate active loans
  • Built analytics and reporting for investors financing the loans
  • Supported regulatory reporting to official entities
  • Developed a partner platform for loan attribution and compensation
Intelligential

Credit management SaaS platform

At Intelligential, product work involved translating lending operations into a platform that could serve multiple financial companies.

  • Launched the first SaaS version based on real operational knowledge from Creze
  • Designed the platform to support companies like Creze and similar lenders
  • Developed an API-first version for clients that wanted direct integration without using the interface layer
  • Built reporting and analytics modules for internal control and legal obligations
Íkualo

Financial platform for migrants in Europe

At Íkualo, product ownership means balancing user experience, compliance, and financial infrastructure constraints.

  • Defined the onboarding process for new users
  • Shaped the mobile app for account management, transactions, cards, and wallet integrations
  • Helped structure the product around daily banking use cases
  • Defined the backoffice used by staff to operate customer accounts in a controlled and secure environment

Product Principles

Product clarity comes through execution The first version makes the conversation real and gives stakeholders something tangible to react to.
The first version is a learning tool Its goal is not completeness. Its goal is to enable movement in the market and informed iteration.
Constraints are part of product design Budget, regulation, team capacity, and operations are not external factors. They shape the product itself.
Operational realities matter A product must work for the end user, but also for the teams that support and operate it every day.

Roles This Experience Fits

  • Product Owner
  • Founding Product Lead
  • Head of Product in execution-heavy environments
  • CTO or technical leadership roles closely involved in product strategy

My work typically sits at the intersection of product, technology, and execution, especially in fintech environments where these areas cannot be separated cleanly.

Product Perspective

I do not see product as the exercise of describing ideal features in abstraction. I see it as the discipline of turning ambiguity into something real, usable, and evolvable under real-world constraints.

— Ethien Salinas
