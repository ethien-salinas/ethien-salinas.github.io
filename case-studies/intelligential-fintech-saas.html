Fintech Case Study

Turning lending operations into fintech SaaS.

How I helped turn real lending workflows into a financial-core SaaS platform used by financial institutions to manage credit operations at scale.

This case is about productizing lending expertise: building configurable credit infrastructure, designing a multi-tenant fintech platform, and creating a SaaS product trusted by regulated institutions.

What this covers

  • Turning operational lending knowledge into a reusable fintech product
  • Designing a configurable multi-tenant financial platform
  • Building a SaaS business for lenders and financial institutions
  • Scaling engineering, product and platform governance from the ground up

Context

After the experience of building a digital lending platform at Creze, it became clear how difficult it was for financial companies to find or build the right systems for credit management.

Origin of the idea

From operating a lender to building infrastructure

At Creze, we experienced first-hand how difficult it was to find a system that could support credit operations properly. The platform we used lacked many capabilities needed to scale the business.

Market opportunity

A financial core for lenders

Intelligential was created to solve that problem: a configurable SaaS platform that could act as a financial core for institutions managing different types of credit products.

In one line, Intelligential was: a credit operating system for financial institutions.

The problem

Building a credit platform internally is not just a technology problem. It requires deep understanding of lending operations, regulatory context, risk processes and financial product structures. For many institutions, that translates into long learning curves, costly internal development and fragile operational systems.

Build cost

Too complex to build from scratch

Financial institutions needed a lending core, but building one internally required specialized domain knowledge and a long implementation cycle.

Product flexibility

Hard to launch new products

Institutions wanted to manage existing credit products and launch new ones through configuration rather than bespoke development every time.

Operational trust

Need for control and reporting

Credit operations require strong reporting, validation, compliance and operational control to support both internal teams and regulators.

Product & strategy

Defined the technology and product strategy, shaping the platform as a reusable financial infrastructure product for lenders.

Architecture

Designed the core architecture of a configurable credit management platform supporting loan lifecycle management, reporting and operational control.

Organization

The product

Intelligential was built as a modular fintech SaaS platform capable of supporting multiple credit products and multiple financial institutions through configuration.

Core engine

Credit management engine

Supported both simple and revolving credit products and acted as the operational core for loan lifecycle management.

Origination

Loan acquisition and approval

Included an origination module and an approval workflow supporting financial risk evaluation and credit decision processes.

Compliance & reporting

Regulatory and operational control

Included AML / PLD capabilities and a reporting system covering strategic, regulatory and compliance needs.

Platform architecture

The platform was designed as a multi-tenant financial SaaS system supporting multiple companies while maintaining strict separation of data and operational integrity.

Architecture approach

Configurable multi-tenant platform

  • API-first design for institutional integrations
  • Modular product structure
  • Support for configurable credit products
  • Separation of client data through logical isolation and selective schema-based patterns for larger institutions
Technology environment

Core stack

  • Vue for frontend applications
  • Node.js and Go for backend services
  • TypeScript and GraphQL
  • AWS infrastructure with EC2, S3, Aurora PostgreSQL and DynamoDB

Strategic challenges

Convert real lending operations into configurable software The product had to support different financial institutions and credit products without becoming a custom-built system for each new client.
Maintain flexibility without losing consistency Clients needed the ability to launch new products through configuration, while the platform still needed reliable and consistent behavior.
Support complex financial calculations at speed Lending operations involve complex operational logic and calculation flows that need to remain fast, predictable and reliable.
Build a regulated SaaS platform from day one Even though Intelligential was not the regulated institution, clients depended on the platform to help support their compliance, controls and reporting requirements.
Balance commercial speed with platform robustness The company needed to move fast commercially, while still building a platform stable enough to earn trust from financial institutions.

Key lessons

Platform thinking from day one matters Building SaaS infrastructure requires designing for scale, configurability and client trust from the very beginning.
SLAs and service reliability are part of the product Once you become the platform provider, uptime, support and service continuity become core parts of the customer value proposition.
Security and governance are part of product trust In financial SaaS, clients do not just buy features. They buy confidence that the platform will help them operate securely and responsibly.
Product and technology evolve together At the beginning, product sat inside technology because speed and architecture were tightly linked. As the platform matured, product became a specialized capability in its own right.
This experience shaped how I built later platforms Many of the lessons from Intelligential later carried into Íkualo, especially around platform thinking, service reliability, security and governance.

Closing perspective

Intelligential was the point where lending know-how turned into financial infrastructure. It taught me how to build not only for one fintech, but for many—creating a platform that had to be configurable, reliable and trusted as part of other institutions’ core operations.

— Ethien Salinas
