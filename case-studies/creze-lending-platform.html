Fintech Case Study

Building a lending platform from scratch.

How I helped take Creze from early pilot loans managed through spreadsheets to a technology-driven lending platform for SMEs in Mexico.

This case is about building the technology backbone of an early fintech: loan origination, risk workflows, operational systems, team growth, and platform evolution.

What this covers

  • Moving from manual lending operations to a production fintech platform
  • Building a credit origination system under early-stage startup constraints
  • Scaling engineering from 5 to 21 people
  • Turning lending operations into structured technology and data flows

Context at the start

Stage
Early-stage fintech with pilot loans already tested
When I joined
November 2016
Team
65 people in the company, 5 in technology
Situation
Around 10 proof-of-concept loans managed operationally through Excel

Context

Creze was an early fintech startup focused on providing working capital loans to small and medium-sized businesses in Mexico.

Business model

Working capital lending

Creze offered loans designed to help existing businesses finance day-to-day operations, rather than startup funding or long-term expansion capital.

Market context

Early fintech in Mexico

The company was founded by Gonzalo Cegarra after seeing the lending model up close in the United States through OnDeck and identifying the opportunity to adapt it to Mexico while the local fintech ecosystem was still emerging.

Loan sizes started at around 50k MXN and, by the time I left, had grown to as much as 1M MXN with collateral guarantees such as founders’ real estate assets.

The challenge

Creze had validated the lending thesis with a small number of pilot loans, but the operational process was still largely manual. There was no production-grade platform capable of supporting scale, operational discipline, or structured risk workflows.

Operations

Manual workflows

Pilot loans were being managed through spreadsheets, which was not sustainable for a digital lending business.

Product

No production platform

The lending process required a real digital flow for registration, document management, risk review, approval and operations.

Data

Messy financial inputs

Bank statements were inconsistent, OCR quality was weak, and open banking did not exist yet, making financial preprocessing and interpretation significantly harder.

My role

I joined Creze in November 2016 after working at Buró de Crédito. My initial role was to take leadership of the engineering team, bring the first real platform into production, and evolve it as the company grew.

Platform delivery

Led the effort to launch the first production version of the lending platform approximately four months after joining.

Team leadership

Structured and expanded the engineering organization as product and operational complexity increased.

Business translation

Worked to understand lending operations end to end and turn those workflows into practical software systems.

The product

The platform supported the full credit lifecycle, starting with digital loan origination and extending into operational and analytical workflows.

Origination

Customer intake and risk review

  • Applicant registration and onboarding
  • Document upload
  • Credit bureau consultation
  • Financial data review
  • Risk evaluation
Credit lifecycle

Approval to collection

  • Loan approval
  • Loan disbursement
  • Loan collection
  • Customer classification
Decision support

Analytics and reporting

  • Operational analytics
  • Financial reporting
  • Compliance and regulatory reporting

Platform & architecture

The initial stack was already in place when I joined, and the first priority was to launch. Once the platform was operating in production, I evolved the system gradually to improve performance, modularity, quality and security.

Core stack

Technology base

  • React and Vue for frontend applications
  • Node.js for backend services
  • PostgreSQL as core database
Infrastructure

Cloud foundation

  • AWS as the cloud platform
  • EC2 for compute
  • S3 for storage
  • Aurora RDS for enterprise-grade data workloads

Strategic decisions

Ship first, optimize next The initial stack was inherited and I kept it in place to get the first production version into the market quickly. Once the platform was operating, I improved components progressively where it made sense.
Evolve the team structure as complexity grows The team moved from generalist full-stack developers toward more specialized roles, especially around backend business logic and, later, data and reporting needs.
Build a stronger system, not just more features As the company grew, the focus expanded beyond feature delivery to quality, modularity, security and operational robustness.
Treat hiring as a strategic technology decision Every role added to the team was a direct leadership choice. I took responsibility not only for hiring, but also for adjusting the organization when people were not evolving at the pace the company required.

Organization & team growth

One of the most important parts of the work was evolving the engineering team from a small startup group into a more structured organization able to support a growing fintech.

Team evolution
From full-stack generalists to stronger backend and data specialization
Backend focus

Business logic and operational systems

As the lending platform matured, backend work became increasingly important because that is where the core lending logic and operational decision-making lived.

Data capability

First analytics and reporting layer

Later in the journey, the team incorporated data-focused capabilities to generate the company’s first financial, compliance and regulatory reporting outputs.

Impact on the business

Technology became essential to Creze’s operating model. Because the company worked as a fully digital lender, the platform was not a support function—it was the business.

Scale

Originations growth

The company grew from early proof-of-concept loans to approximately 500k MXN in monthly loan originations by the time I left.

Efficiency

Operational support for risk

The platform helped preprocess financial information such as bank statements, making risk analysis more efficient despite inconsistent source data.

Visibility

Reporting and decision support

Technology enabled operational, strategic, financial and regulatory reporting needed to run the lending business day to day.

Marketing

Better digital acquisition

As I understood the business more deeply, I also contributed to digital marketing thinking and helped support more effective acquisition strategies.

Operating model

From spreadsheets to fintech platform

The company moved from managing a handful of pilot loans manually to operating through a real lending platform capable of supporting scale.

Key lessons

This was my first true exposure to startup execution Creze marked my transition from technical leadership in larger organizations to building technology in an early-stage company where product, operations and business were tightly linked.
Building fintech means understanding operations end to end I stopped seeing technology as feature delivery only and began understanding lending as a complete operating system: risk, data, disbursement, collection and reporting.
Platform thinking starts with operational reality The deep exposure to how lending actually worked became the foundation for Intelligential, where those learnings later evolved into a fintech SaaS platform.
My own leadership had to evolve One of the biggest challenges was broadening my perspective beyond programming and learning to think in terms of complete systems, organizational design and business impact.

Closing perspective

Creze was the point where my career moved from technical delivery to full fintech platform building. It taught me how lending businesses really operate, how technology becomes the backbone of the company, and how startup execution changes the role of a technology leader completely.

— Ethien Salinas
